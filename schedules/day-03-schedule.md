# Day 3 Schedule: Group Comparison And Association

Assumed class block: 3 hours.

## Main Goal

Students can compare groups, plot differences directly, and describe association without causal overclaiming.

## Schedule

| Time | Segment | Materials | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:10 | Warm-up and Day 2 recap | Verbal | Ask: count, proportion, distribution, or rank? |
| 0:10-0:25 | Concept setup: comparison and association | `slides/day-03-comparison-and-association.qmd` | Keep this short. |
| 0:25-0:55 | Grouped summaries | `modules/04_group-comparison/01_grouped-summaries.qmd` | Compare distributions before summaries. |
| 0:55-1:15 | Difference plots | `modules/04_group-comparison/02_difference-plots.qmd` | Emphasize what zero means. |
| 1:15-1:25 | Break |  | Buffer. |
| 1:25-1:55 | Scatterplots, smoothing, and groups | `modules/05_association/01_scatterplots-smoothing-and-groups.qmd` | Direction, form, strength, groups. |
| 1:55-2:20 | Model-output plots | `modules/05_association/02_model-output-plots.qmd` | Keep this conceptual and light. |
| 2:20-2:55 | Student practice | `practice/templates/04_group-comparison/`, `practice/templates/05_association/` | Require one plot and one cautious interpretation. |
| 2:55-3:00 | Wrap | Verbal | Preview change, space, flow. |

## If Time Runs Short

Treat model-output plots as a short preview. Do not cut scatterplots and group comparison.

## Instructor Priorities

- Students do not default to bar charts for every group comparison.
- Students label differences clearly.
- Students use association language, not causal language.
- Students see model plots as summaries with assumptions.
