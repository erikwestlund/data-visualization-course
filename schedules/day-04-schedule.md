# Day 4 Schedule: Change, Space, And Flow

Assumed class block: 3 hours.

## Main Goal

Students choose visual forms for ordered, spatial, and process questions and justify why the form fits the task.

## Schedule

| Time | Segment | Materials | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:10 | Warm-up and recap | Verbal | Ask students to name the comparison in a plot. |
| 0:10-0:25 | Concept setup: order, place, process | `slides/day-04-change-space-flow.qmd` | Frame by data structure. |
| 0:25-0:55 | Time trends and small multiples | `modules/06_change/01_time-trends-and-small-multiples.qmd` | Facets over spaghetti lines. |
| 0:55-1:15 | Before-after and trajectories | `modules/06_change/02_before-after-and-trajectories.qmd` | Individual versus group change. |
| 1:15-1:25 | Break |  | Buffer. |
| 1:25-1:50 | Geographic comparison without maps | `modules/07_space/01_geographic-comparison-without-maps.qmd` | Non-map comparisons are central. |
| 1:50-2:10 | Flow and attrition | `modules/08_flow/01_flow-and-attrition.qmd` | Sample construction and retained counts. |
| 2:10-2:25 | Process diagrams | `modules/08_flow/02_process-diagrams.qmd` | Short Mermaid demo. |
| 2:25-2:55 | Student practice | `practice/templates/06_change/`, `practice/templates/07_space/`, `practice/templates/08_flow/` | Students choose one path. |
| 2:55-3:00 | Wrap | Verbal | Preview polish/project studio. |

## If Time Runs Short

Skip or shorten process diagrams. Preserve time trends and non-map spatial comparison.

## Instructor Priorities

- Students preserve meaningful order.
- Students understand maps are not always the best spatial comparison.
- Students can explain sample attrition visually.
- Students justify plot choice, not just code completion.
